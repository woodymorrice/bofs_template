Whistled Intervals Study

Consent to Participate

Thank you for agreeing to participate in our study. Please read the following information carefully before proceeding.

Researchers:

Woody Morrice, B.Sc., woody.morrice@usask.ca

Purpose and Objective of the Research:

The goal of this study is to investigate the ability of participants to reproduce a candidate set of pitch-agnostic whistled intervals. You will be given an opportunity to practice the set of intervals with guidance, then you will be tested for how accurately you can reproduce each interval without guidance. After you have completeted the interactive portion of the study, you will be asked to fill out a questionnaire assessing several metrics about each interval such as comfort and effort, followed by a preference questionnaire.

Procedures:

  • The study will consist of a single task, and the entire experiment will take approximately 20 minutes.
  • For the task, you will be provided with a visualization to guide your while you whistle the intervals during training, however the guidance will be removed when you reach the testing phase so that we can properly assess how well participants are able to reproduce the intervals without guidance.
  • After completing each iteration of the task, you will be asked to describe why you chose that specific bug report.
  • Data from the task and questionnaires will be recorded.
  • No video will be recorded during the session, however we will be recording an audio clip of each trial for further analysis.
  • Please feel free to ask any questions regarding the procedures and goals of the study or your role.

Potential Risks

  • There are no known or anticipated risks to you by participating in this research.
  • You will be asked to carry out tasks in a mouse-and-windows environment; you may feel minor fatigue from these tasks, and you will be given the opportunity to rest between tasks.
  • You will be observed by the experimenter during the tasks. The goal of the study is to evaluate the strategies and techniques employed rather than your performance, and any problems that you have with the tasks are primarily due to the design of said strategies and techniques. If you feel any anxiety or discomfort during the study, please inform the experimenter.
  • After the study, you will be given more information about the purpose and goals of the study, and there will be time for you to ask questions about the research.

Benefits

  • The main benefit to you is in contributing to our understanding of whistled intervals. This information can be used to design more efficient hands-free interaction techniques.

Confidentiality

  • The data collected from this study may be used in articles for publication in journals and conference proceedings.
  • All personal and identifying data will be kept confidential. Your name or any personal identifiable data will not be associated with the data gathered, and data will be reported in aggregate form so that it will not be possible to identify individuals. For any presentation of comments, confidentiality will be preserved by using participant ID numbers.
  • Consent forms will be stored separately from the data so that it will not be possible to associate a name with any given set of responses. The informed consent form and all research data will be kept in a secure and confidential location, in accordance with University policy, for 5 years post publication.

Storage of Data

  • Any electronic data will be stored in a password-protected database that is located on a secure server in the Department of Computer Science.
  • Any paper forms or data will be stored in a locked cabinet in a locked office.
  • All data will be stored for a minimum of five years. After this period, all data will be deleted beyond recovery, and paper records will be securely destroyed.
  • Consent forms will be stored separately from the data so that it will not be possible to associate a name with any given set of responses.

Right to Withdraw

  • Your participation is voluntary and you can answer only those questions that you are comfortable with. You may withdraw from the research project for any reason, at any time without explanation or penalty of any sort.
  • Should you wish to withdraw during the study session, inform the experimenter at any time during the study. If you wish to withdraw after the session is complete, please email the experimenter (at the email address used to schedule this session).
  • If you choose to withdraw, your data will be deleted from the study and all records of your participation will be destroyed. Note that at a certain point, data will have been aggregated and presented in publications, and at this point it will no longer be possible to remove your data from the study.
  • Whether you choose to participate or not will have no effect on your position (e.g., employment, academic status, access to services) or how you will be treated.
  • Your right to withdraw data from the study will apply until data has been aggregated and presented in publications; at this point it will no longer be possible to remove your data from the study.

Follow up

  • To obtain a summary of results from the study, please send an email message to the experimenter or the principal investigator.
